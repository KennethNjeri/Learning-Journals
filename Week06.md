## Content Insights
The daily standup is a short, timeboxed meeting (about 15 minutes) where the development team self-organizes by answering three questions: what they did yesterday, what they will do today, and any obstacles they face.
Team members stand during the meeting to keep it brief and focused, and only developers actively contribute; others like Scrum Masters and product owners listen and may answer questions but should not lead the meeting.
It's important to avoid turning the standup into a traditional status meeting driven by project managers, as this undermines self-organization and slows progress. The Scrum Master helps maintain this discipline and encourages team interaction.
